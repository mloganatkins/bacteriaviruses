Bacteria Life Cycle

Lag Phase

The bacteria do not grow but try to adjust to their environment and metabolize. They produce required substances for division like amino acids and vitamins.

Log Phase

Bacteria multiply quickly when in favorable conditions. Bacteria double in size during the "generation time" and undergo the process of binary fission.

Stationary Phase

This is a phase of decline in bacteria growth. Growth and death rates are equal during this phase. What happens during this phase depends on environmental factors.

Death Phase

Last stage where bacteria lose the ability to reproduce.

Virus Life Cycle

Lytic

A virus enters a cell, makes a copy of itself and causes the cell to burst which releases the virus into the cell.

Attachment

The phage attaches to the surface of the host.

Penatration

The viral DNA enters the host cell through contraction of tail sheath which inject the viral DNA into the cell.

Biosynthesis

Phage DNA replicates and phage proteins are made by taking over the bacterium's metabolism.

Maturation

New, complete phage particles are assembled.

Release

The cell reptures, releasing the newly made phages.
